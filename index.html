Solar system

Mercury

Distance from the sun: 0.466

Orbital period: 88 days

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the Sun's planets.

Venus

Distance from the sun: 0.72 AU

Orbital period: 225 days

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. As the second-brightest natural object in Earth's night sky after the Moon, Venus can cast shadows and can be, on rare occasion, visible to the naked eye in broad daylight.

Earth

Distance from the sun: 1 AU

Orbital period: 365 days

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. While large volumes of water can be found throughout the Solar System, only Earth sustains liquid surface water.

Mars

Distance from the sun: 1.52 AU

Orbital period: 687 days

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. In English, Mars carries a name of the Roman god of war, and is often referred to as the "Red Planet".

Jupiter

Distance from the sun: 5.2 AU

Orbital period: 12 years

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass one-thousandth that of the Sun, but two-and-a-half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined.

Saturn

Distance from the sun: 9.5 AU

Orbital period: 29 years

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius about nine times that of Earth.

Uranus

Distance from the sun: 19.2 AU

Orbital period: 84 years

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System. It is named after the Greek god of the sky, Uranus.

Neptune

Distance from the sun: 30.1 AU

Orbital period: 165 years

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet.
